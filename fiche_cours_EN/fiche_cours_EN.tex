\maketitle

This course provides a broad and accessible introduction to quantum computing, with practical demonstrations using IBM's \textbf{Qiskit} ecosystem. It is designed for participants from diverse backgrounds such as students, developers, and professionals with basic knowledge of mathematics (vectors, matrices) and Python programming. We will learn how to implement elementary quantum circuits and run them on real quantum computers made available to the general public by IBM.

\section*{Content}

This course will cover the following topics:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.4em]
\item Classical and quantum computing: advantages and motivations;
\item Essential notions of quantum mechanics applied to computing: qubit, quantum logic gates, bra--ket notation, superposition states, measurement, \ldots
\item Multi-qubit quantum circuits: quantum entanglement, manipulation of logic gates;
\item Programming quantum circuits with the \textbf{Qiskit} library: preparation of an entangled state and quantum teleportation;
\item Overview of a more advanced quantum algorithm with \textbf{Qiskit}: Grover's algorithm;
\item Execution of circuits on a real quantum computer;
\end{itemize}

In addition to these topics, other subjects may be covered depending on participants' interests:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.4em]
\item Overview of hardware platforms for quantum computing: existing technologies and current limitations;
\item CHSH and Bell inequalities: interpretation of quantum superposition;
\item Complex numbers for quantum computing;
\item Evolution of quantum states and the impossibility of copying a quantum state;
\item Quantum key distribution.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Learning Outcomes}

At the end of this training, participants will have:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.4em]
\item A basic understanding of quantum computing;
\item The ability to implement simple quantum circuits with Qiskit;
\item The ability to run algorithms on IBM's publicly accessible quantum computers.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Training Method}

This training alternates between lectures and practical demonstrations.

\section*{Prerequisites}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.4em]
\item Basic mathematical knowledge of vectors and matrices;
\item Python programming;
\end{itemize}

Recommended reading in linear algebra (vectors, matrices):
